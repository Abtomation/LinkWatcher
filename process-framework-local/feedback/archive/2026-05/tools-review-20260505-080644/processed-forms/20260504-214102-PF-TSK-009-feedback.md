## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- **Step 2 unilateral rejection authority** for IMP-706 — the "no human checkpoint needed at this gate" clause is essential when a problem is clearly absent. I still surfaced the rationale to the user before marking, but the option to fast-path was important.
- **Compressed Step 13 format** (PF-IMP-701, recently added) — used for IMP-712 where Step 11 sweep was clean and there was no deviation. Avoided restating an already-approved one-line plan.
- **Step 6 conciseness rule for all-favorable evaluations** — IMP-712 used the one-line summary instead of the full table. Saved several paragraphs of boilerplate.
- **Risk-class differentiation at Step 10** — Medium for IMP-704 (data-touching SQL UPDATE) triggered backup discipline; Low for IMP-712 (one-clause edit) skipped per-change checkpoints. Matched effort to risk correctly.
- **CONSOLIDATION_MAPPING explicit-pair design** for IMP-704 — hardcoding the 15 (from→to) pairs in the script (rather than a heuristic that could surprise later) made the consolidation auditable and the dry-run output match Step 6's predicted counts exactly.

### What could be improved
- **Minimum-Viability gate phrasing**: triggers identically whether MV="Yes because a simpler change exists" or MV="Yes because the proposal IS the minimum". The latter case forces a slightly artificial comparison against an even-smaller "do nothing" alternative. Real-world impact is low (just produces a one-extra-bullet checkpoint message), but the criterion-vs-gate semantics could be tightened.
- **Bash quoting collision** with apostrophes in -ValidationNotes (single-quoted bash string can't contain `'`). CLAUDE.md's "fallback temp-file pattern" handled it cleanly. Single occurrence; not worth a callout.

### Specific suggestions
- (Observation only — single occurrence): MV-gate phrasing could distinguish "simpler version exists" from "proposal IS minimum". Not filing as IMP — single occurrence and the workaround (interpret the gate as "compare to do-nothing alternative") is workable.
