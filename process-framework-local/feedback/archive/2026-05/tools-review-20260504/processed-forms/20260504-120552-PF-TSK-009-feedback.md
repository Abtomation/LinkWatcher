## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- Step 6 explicit-alternative requirement: forced articulating the radical option (preventative pre-commit dry-run for IMP-675; outer-success-removal for IMP-691; rejection rationale for IMP-679) before defaulting to MVP. Useful framing even when MVP wins.
- Step 11 silent-housekeeping rule for linked docs: discovered the contradiction between PF-TSK-026 Step 1(c) and the framework-extension-customization-guide.md callout for IMP-679. The "mention only if substantive" filter kept the Step 13 checkpoint focused.
- Risk-class gating in Step 10: correctly skipped per-change sub-checkpoints for all three Low-risk changes; would have triggered them for High-risk.
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 atomic state transitions: 6/6 invocations clean.

### What could be improved
- The Pilot vs. Full Rollout (Step 4.5 reference) and Data-Driven Validation gates were N/A for all three IMPs but still required a read-and-dismiss. For pure content updates these gates can be assumed N/A by default.
- "Project" vs. "framework" terminology in PF-TSK-026 Step 1 itself was ambiguous (the IMP-679 work surfaced this); the human partner had to correct my proposed wording. The task definition has the same ambiguity in (b) that I left untouched ("project's current workflow") because (b) plausibly refers to the broader project, not the framework being extended.
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 verbose output (~9 lines × 6 invocations) — already covered by the open PF-IMP-671 -Quiet flag proposal.

### Specific suggestions
- None new. The above observations don't yet warrant new IMPs by themselves: the Pilot/Data-Validation N/A overhead is a single-session observation and conflicts with the "no-anti-pattern-callouts-from-single-occurrences" rule. The "project vs framework" ambiguity in PF-TSK-026 (b) is real but I'm intentionally not proposing a follow-up IMP per the "observe-before-broadening" rule — surface as observation only.
