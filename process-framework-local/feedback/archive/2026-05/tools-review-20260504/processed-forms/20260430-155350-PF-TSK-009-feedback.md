## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- Step 6's solution-exploration directive forced both an MVP variant and a radical alternative onto the table — even though I framed the radical poorly, the structure exposed the choice for human review.
- The data-driven validation gate on PF-IMP-673 produced the drift table (8/16 entries off, BM-008 100x off) that materially shifted the recommended approach.
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 atomic transitions (status + summary count + history entry + frontmatter date in one call) eliminated the file-coordination class of errors.
- Step 12 silent change logging worked: feedback_db.py's `--new-tool` gate caught two unregistered tool_doc_ids before silent typos could corrupt analytics.
- Step 11 silent housekeeping check on PF-IMP-655 surfaced the active references (script, doc map, usage guide, task registry) that would have needed updating if the diff had structural impact — none did, kept silent per directive.

### What could be improved
- **Default-rejecting the radical alternative**: I labeled "parse MD directly" as "NOT recommended" on PF-IMP-673's first checkpoint and recommended an MVP data-fix. The human partner correctly redirected: dual-maintenance was the structural problem; fixing the values is treating the symptom. The Step 6 directive ("weighting the radical option by its benefit ceiling rather than its effort cost") was in the task definition but I didn't apply it. This isn't a task-definition gap; it's a behavioural drift on my part.
- **Unit-test scope confusion**: I proposed adding `test/automated/unit/test_performance_db_parser.py` as part of PF-IMP-673 implementation. The human partner clarified that `test/automated/` is reserved for LinkWatcher product code; framework-shared scripts under `process-framework/` are validated via smoke runs at the Step 13 checkpoint. This rule is not visible in PF-TSK-009 or in the script-development guide.
- **feedback_db.py `--new-tool` per invocation**: When a multi-tool change set has 2+ first-time-registered tools, `--new-tool` is required separately for each. Reasonable today; would be friction for larger change sets.

### Specific suggestions
- **Add a callout to PF-TSK-009 Step 6** clarifying that the radical alternative should not be presented as "NOT recommended" by default — operator should evaluate it on benefit ceiling, not on regression-risk to a single prior decision (e.g., my framing "regresses TD245" treated TD245's specific implementation as inviolable rather than reconsidering the goal). Concrete wording: "When the radical alternative would alter a prior architectural decision, evaluate whether the prior decision's *goal* (not its specific implementation) is preserved by the alternative — re-examine before defaulting to 'preserve prior'."
- **Add a callout to PF-TSK-009 Step 10** (or a referenced guide) clarifying test scope for framework-script changes: "Changes to scripts under `process-framework/` are validated via direct invocation/smoke testing at Step 13. Do not propose adding pytest test files under `test/automated/` for framework script changes — `test/automated/` is reserved for product feature tests in adopter projects."
- **feedback_db.py log-change**: consider accepting `--new-tool` once per invocation to register multiple tools in a single call (mild — current behaviour is correct, just a paper cut at scale).
