## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- Step 2 evaluation table forced a course-correction on IMP-634 (after the human partner pushed back) and prevented a similar over-engineering on IMP-635 (chose Option D — deletion — instead of the IMP's literal rename).
- Step 11 linked-doc sweep caught downstream references in 2 of 3 IMPs (IMP-634 had 4 hits across two files; IMP-635 was clean; IMP-687 was clean).
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1's auto-summary-count and auto-history-entry eliminated manual bookkeeping risk across 6 invocations.
- Step 12's "log tool change in feedback DB" placement (after edits, before final checkpoint) captured changes at the verification point.
- Smoke test pattern from PF-IMP-631 (PSParser tokenize + Get-Help -Full + WhatIf invocation) caught nothing this time but provided confidence — IMP-687 cleanly verified the new ValidateScript via a concrete 2050-char rejection test.

### What could be improved
- Step 5 + Step 13 checkpoint sequencing felt redundant for trivial doc-only IMPs (IMP-635). Not a true gap — Step 8's skip clause already handles the most common redundancy — but for one-bullet edits, Step 13 sometimes asks the same question that Step 5 already answered.
- feedback_db.py has no edit/delete for change records. Typo recovery is functional via corrective entry but creates two-row noise.

### Specific suggestions
*Documented only — not registered as PF-IMP per template instruction.*
- Consider explicit guidance for "Step 13 reduces to confirmation" when no Step 11 hits found AND change is one bullet/line. Current flow runs full Step 13 every time; a short-circuit ("confirm sweep was clean — proceeding") would save a turn for trivial cases.
- feedback_db.py: a `correct-change <change-id> --field <field> --value <value>` subcommand for typo fixes would be cleaner than corrective-entry pattern. Low priority — typos are rare.
