## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- **Skeleton-then-fill ordering for review summary** (Step 11 before Step 12) made SourceLink references in batch IMP creation friction-free.
- **40-form session limit + oversized-group exception** correctly drove the Option B split decision at the Step 5 checkpoint.
- **BatchFile mode for New-ProcessImprovement.ps1** registered 26 IMPs in one invocation — vastly preferable to 26 single-call commands.
- **extract_ratings.py + feedback_db.py record chain** integrates cleanly with the archive folder structure and required no manual JSON construction.
- **Step 6 "Unit of Analysis" callout** ("each distinct actionable change is its own IMP regardless of theme grouping") was load-bearing for granularity decisions during batch construction.
- **Sequential form reading discipline** caught patterns batch-scanning would miss (e.g., the cluster of Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 ergonomic gaps surfaced piece by piece across 9 PF-TSK-009 forms).

### What could be improved
- **Step 14 archive procedure is not concurrency-safe** — a parallel session creating/moving feedback forms during my mv left the archive folder with extra forms. Recovery worked but added unplanned time. A pre-archive snapshot, session-unique subfolder, or "lock the feedback-forms folder for the duration of mv" pattern would harden this.
- **No "diminishing returns" prompt for large same-task groups** — by form 5 of the 17 PF-TSK-009 forms, patterns were stabilizing. Sequential reading still added value (each form contributed at least one observation), but a mid-group pivot to "scan for novel observations only" would save context and time on large clusters.
- **Triple-🚨 banner in New-FeedbackForm.ps1** (Tool 6 above) — confirmed again as recurring noise across the New-* family. Per user "observe before broadening" memory, deferred.

### Specific suggestions
- **PF-TSK-010 Step 14 (Archive)**: Add a sub-step "if the feedback folder may be active in parallel sessions, create the archive folder with a unique session ID (timestamp) and verify the move count matches the intended count before proceeding to ratings extraction". Would have caught this session's 74-vs-40 mismatch immediately.
- **PF-TSK-010 large-group analysis**: Consider a note under "Analysis quality over speed" — "for task groups exceeding 10 forms, the first 5-7 forms typically establish the pattern set; subsequent forms can be scanned for novel observations only, with full reading for any form raising new themes". Captures the diminishing-returns reality without sacrificing rigor.

## Additional Context

- **Concurrent Session 2 archive interference**: Session 2 (PF-REV-033) was running in parallel and completed its archive of 34 forms into the same `tools-review-20260429/processed-forms/` folder roughly when my Session 1 mv ran.
