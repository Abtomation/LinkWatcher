## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- **Step 3 minimum-viability gate** caught real design alternatives: IMP-650 had three viable approaches (`-ItemCount` parameter, comma-separated `-DebtItemId` list, hybrid); IMP-674 had a "just reject — already self-evident from tracking file" candidate worth surfacing.
- **Conciseness rule (Step 3)** appropriately suppressed full-table presentation for the two LOW-priority IMPs where all criteria rated favorably.
- **Step 11 silent-housekeeping framing** is exactly right — the IMP-650 linked-doc check found two substantive references (lightweight-path L1, usage guide) that I updated without bloating the human's view.
- **Risk-classified Step 10** let low-risk additive changes batch-execute without per-change sub-checkpoints — appropriate proportionality.
- **Step 6's "do not enumerate ideas you discarded"** kept checkpoint presentations focused on surviving options.

### What could be improved
- **feedback_db.py `log-change --batch` rejects the entire batch if any entry has an unknown tool_doc_id**. During IMP-650 a 4-entry batch failed atomically because one tool was legitimately new (`lightweight-refactoring-plan-template.md`); fallback was 4 individual calls. Per-entry `--new-tool` in the batch JSON, or an `--allow-new-tools` flag, would let mixed batches succeed.

### Specific suggestions
- feedback_db.py: support per-entry new-tool acknowledgment in batch mode (e.g., add `"new_tool": true` field per JSON entry, or accept `--allow-new-tools` on the batch invocation that applies the `--new-tool` semantics to any unknown IDs in the batch).
