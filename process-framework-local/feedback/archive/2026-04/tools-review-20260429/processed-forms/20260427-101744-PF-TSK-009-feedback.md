## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- **Step 11 grep sweep is high-ROI** — finding 2 bonus IMPs (PF-IMP-599, PF-IMP-600) from a sweep on PF-IMP-578 validated the cost of the mandatory step many times over. PF-IMP-335 had previously claimed "zero remaining -Category references" but missed both PF-TSK-083 and PF-TSK-066 — Step 11 catches what prior agents miss.
- **Step 2 evaluation table forces honest classification** — the "Framework Fit" criterion explicitly notes "If the fix requires creating new artifacts rather than modifying existing tooling, route to PF-TSK-048/PF-TSK-001 instead." This caught the PF-IMP-599 → PF-TSK-014 delegation correctly.
- **"Delegated" status** in the IMP tracker is a real-world fit. Having it as a discrete status (not Deferred, not Completed) made PF-IMP-599's lifecycle traceable.
- **Three-IMP session limit** — felt appropriate. Context fatigue was visible by IMP 3.

### What could be improved
- The dual checkpoint structure (Step 5 problem+approach, Step 8 approve approach) duplicates content for content-only fixes. PF-IMP-594 already proposes a fast-path.
- For IMPs delegated to PF-TSK-014 inline (same session), the lifecycle "Needs Prioritization → InProgress → Delegated → ... → InProgress → Completed" felt awkward. The IMP got marked Delegated, then later Completed via its own Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 invocation. A "Resolved by delegation, target task X" intermediate state may be cleaner.
- Update-TechDebt.ps1 examples drift in task-doc files (proven by PF-IMP-335 missing PF-TSK-083 and PF-TSK-066, plus the in-script defect in New-DebtItem.ps1). A periodic validation pass that diffs example invocations against actual `-?` synopsis would catch future drift mechanically.

### Specific suggestions
- **(Already-filed)** PF-IMP-594 — content-update fast-path merging Steps 5 and 8.
- **(Already-filed)** PF-IMP-593 — document the New-ProcessImprovement.ps1 -Description 500-char cap.
- **(Already-filed)** PF-IMP-591 — feedback_db.py --list-tools subcommand for canonical tool ID validation.
- **(New observation, not filed)** Consider adding a `-LookupTaskByExample <ScriptPath>` validator script that walks all task definitions and grep-checks whether their fenced ```bash/```powershell example invocations match the script's `-?` synopsis. Would have caught PF-IMP-578, PF-IMP-600, PF-IMP-599 mechanically.
