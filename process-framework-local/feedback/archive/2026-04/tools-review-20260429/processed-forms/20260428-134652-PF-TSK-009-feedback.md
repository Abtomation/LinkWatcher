## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- The Step 2 evaluation table + Minimum Viability gate together drove correct decisions on all 3 IMPs: proceed (594), reject on weak data (596), scope-trim (608). The framework prevented two over-engineering paths I would have considered without the gate.
- The IMP-594 fast-path edit self-tested mid-session: 596 and 608 both presented multiple Step 5 approaches → Step 7 effectively used → Step 8 not skipped. The mechanical trigger (Step 7 used / not used) worked as designed.
- `feedback_db.py list-tools --filter` (added by IMP-591) cleanly disambiguated tool IDs before logging — exactly its intended use.
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 Rejected status with em-dash Impact preserved the "Rejected" state in tracking history with full rationale.

### What could be improved
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 ValidateSet for `-Impact` accepts `HIGH;MEDIUM;LOW;—` but rejects `N/A`. When transitioning to `Rejected`, "N/A" is the natural mental model; the em-dash is non-obvious. Either accept "N/A" as alias for "—", or have the error message suggest "—" specifically when Status=Rejected.
- The IMP-596 source attribution (PF-FEE-967 in the tracking entry) was a misattribution — the actual source form was 20260417-152253. Surfaced during Step 3 source review. No corrective action needed (the IMP was rejected anyway), but it's a reminder that PF-IMP-XXX entries' "Notes" column citations should be verified, not trusted blindly.
