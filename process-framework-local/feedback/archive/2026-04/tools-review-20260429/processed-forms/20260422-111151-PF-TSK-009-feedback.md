## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- PF-TSK-009 Step 2 evaluation table (especially the "Still Valid?" first row) as a premise-validation gate — prevented speculative remediation of an already-resolved problem.
- Surface 15 + Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 closed-loop: the detector that files the IMP is the same tool that validates its continued validity.
- Rejection path clearly documented in Step 5 ("Valid outcomes" block).
- IMP-575 was filed with a self-contained validation command in its Notes column — this made the "Still Valid?" check a single invocation instead of a reasoning exercise.

### What could be improved
- Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 requires `-Impact` for Rejected transitions, but the valid values are HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW/—. "Rejected" semantically implies no impact, so "NONE" or an implicit default would be more intuitive. Discovering `-Impact "—"` required 2 failed invocations.
- PF-TSK-009 Step 15 example shows the Completed invocation but not the Rejected invocation. A second example line for Rejected (including the `-Impact "—"` workaround) would eliminate the trial-and-error.

### Specific suggestions
- **Script UX**: Consider making `-Impact` optional for Rejected status (default to "—"), or accepting "NONE" as an alias in the ValidateSet. (Captured in "Process Improvements to Consider" below.)
- **Task doc**: Add a Rejected-status example under Step 15 alongside the Completed example:
  ```powershell
  # For rejected IMPs (no-longer-valid, reclassified, etc.):
  Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 -ImprovementId "IMP-XXX" -NewStatus "Rejected" -Impact "—" -ValidationNotes "Why it was rejected."
  ```
