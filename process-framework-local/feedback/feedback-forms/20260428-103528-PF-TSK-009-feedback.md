## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- **Step 2 evaluation table** as a "Still Valid?" gate — saved IMP-576 from over-scope by surfacing that 2 of 3 claimed gaps were already solved in the docs.
- **Step 5 minimum-viability comparison** — pushed the explicit Option B (1 callout) vs Option A (3 edits) discussion before committing.
- **Step 9 bulk-pattern guidance** ("verify with grep-based checks") — explicitly authorized `replace_all` across 25 files for IMP-611, eliminating the artificial pressure to do per-file checkpoints.
- **Step 11 hit list table** — turning the grep sweep into a per-file disposition list made the Step 13 checkpoint inspectable rather than a blob of text.
- **Bundling side findings into IMP-611** — the user explicitly asked for this; the workflow accommodated by absorbing into IMP scope rather than spawning new IMPs that would exceed the 3-IMP session limit.

### What could be improved
- **Mid-IMP scope expansion guidance**: PF-TSK-009 doesn't explicitly handle "side findings discovered during implementation that are tightly coupled — bundle into current IMP, or file separately?" The user had to direct it; codifying the heuristic ("bundle if tightly coupled to same logical change AND would otherwise exceed session limit") would help.
- **Trivial-IMP overhead**: For IMP-609 (single-line frontmatter add), the full Step 5/8/13/14 checkpoint sequence felt heavy for a sub-15-minute change. PF-IMP-594 (already filed) targets this with a content-update fast-path.

### Specific suggestions
- Already filed in this session (PF-IMP-594 content-update fast-path, PF-IMP-593 description cap doc, PF-IMP-584 verbose banner, PF-IMP-582 -ToolCount, PF-IMP-587 pre-populate audit report placeholders). No new tooling suggestions surface from this session beyond what's queued.

## Additional Context

### Task-specific challenges
- **Scope reframing mid-checkpoint**: For IMP-576 the user reframed the framing at Step 5 (project-state vs doc-only). My initial Step 5 used the IMP description's framing literally, which led to over-scope. The user's reframe was correct and non-obvious from the docs. The Human Intervention Log entry #1 captures this as a doc gap.
- **Scope expansion authorization**: For IMP-611 the bundled scope (~60 edits) is significantly larger than a typical PF-TSK-009 IMP. The Step 9 bulk-pattern note authorized this implicitly, but a session-limit-aware "bundle vs file new IMPs" decision wasn't pre-codified — see What Could Be Improved.
- **Pre-existing inconsistencies surfaced during implementation**: Reading the script revealed orthogonal drift (Validate-AuditReport.ps1 regex backward-compat alias, "Audit Approved with Dependencies" orphan status, broken Start-AutomationMenu.ps1 line) that wasn't part of IMP-611. Filed these as 3 new IMPs (612, 613, 614) at finalization rather than absorbing — kept IMP-611 scope honest.

### Integration with other tools
- **Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1** integrated with **Update-ProcessImprovement-tracking.md** (the tracked file) seamlessly — every transition was a single command, summary counters auto-recalculated.
- **feedback_db.py log-change** integrated with **doc-IDs**: most files have IDs (TE-STA-002/003, PF-TSK-030, PF-TEM-039) that the DB accepts directly. Files without IDs (e.g., performance-test-tracking.md before IMP-609) had to be logged by file path — slightly less canonical but functional. PF-IMP-591 (already filed) addresses this with a `--list-tools` subcommand for canonical-ID verification.
- **Validate-IdRegistry.ps1** caught nothing related to my changes (TE-STA-003 allocation was clean), but surfaced unrelated pre-existing issues (8 wrong-prefix files, 2 invalid TD directories) that I noted and skipped.
