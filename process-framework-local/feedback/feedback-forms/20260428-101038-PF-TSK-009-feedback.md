## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- **Step 2 evaluation table** caught IMP-580 details upfront (Approach A vs B trade-off was visible early; user chose B with full understanding).
- **Two-checkpoint pattern** in IMP-582: Step 5 problem analysis exposed my initial overengineered approach; user pushback (`-ToolCount` was unnecessary) reshaped the plan; Step 8 detailed plan was approved cleanly.
- **Step 11 grep sweep + Step 12 feedback DB log** are quick, mechanical steps that produce auditable artifacts.
- **Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1** is bulletproof — six invocations (3× InProgress + 3× Completed) all atomic and self-recalculating.
- **feedback_db.py batch mode** (`--batch -` with stdin JSON) collapsed IMP-580's 3 tool changes into one invocation.
- **Per-IMP atomic close-out** (mark Completed before next claim) ensured each IMP was fully wrapped before the next started.

### What could be improved
- **Step 2 evaluation table lacks an explicit "Minimum Viability" check.** IMP-582 evaluated Favorable across all current criteria, yet I produced an overengineered first proposal. Required human pushback ("I fear we are overengineering"). PF-IMP-581 (already pending) is the right fix.
- **Tool ID convention for scripts without formal IDs is implicit.** Step 12 says "log tool change" but doesn't specify what `--tool` value to use when the changed tool is a `.ps1` script. I had to grep prior entries / try `query --tool` to confirm convention. PF-IMP-591 (`--list-tools`) and PF-IMP-601 (PF-SCR prefix) both pending.
- **Two-checkpoint pattern is heavy for pure content updates.** IMP-583 (single step insertion, no real design alternatives) went through Step 5 approach + Step 8 plan with little new content in the second. PF-IMP-594 (content-update fast-path merging Steps 5+8) is the right scope.

### Specific suggestions
- Confirm-then-implement: when IMPs land in tools-review backlogs in clusters (this session: 4 of the 7+ IMPs from tools-review-20260422 are now done — IMP-578 by 2026-04-22 history, IMP-580/582/583 this session), the natural follow-up is the remaining ones (IMP-581, IMP-584–587).
