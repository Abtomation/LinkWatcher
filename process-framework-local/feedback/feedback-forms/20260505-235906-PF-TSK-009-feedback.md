## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well
- Step 3 evaluation gates (Minimum Viability, Root-Cause Targeting, Data-Driven Validation) demonstrably added value: PF-IMP-740 expanded from 1-template fix to root-cause cleanup of unused field across 5 templates + 2 scripts; PF-IMP-743 was correctly rejected as written (opt-out flag for fixable errors) and counter-fixed instead.
- Step 13's "Skip option for low-risk + no-deviation" relief valve avoided checkpoint-fatigue on PF-IMP-747.
- The 3-IMP session limit triggered cleanly and prevented quality degradation on the third (most complex) IMP.
- Validate-StateTracking.ps1's stable error fingerprints made before/after delta verification trivial — exactly the workflow PF-IMP-743 was trying to engineer with a flag.
- Surfacing scope-creep early during PF-IMP-743 (3 layers deep into derivative findings) and asking before committing further was the right call; the user agreed with the stop-and-file-follow-up recommendation.

### What could be improved
- Parameter naming inconsistency between New-ProcessImprovement.ps1 (`-Source`, `-Priority`) and Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1 (`-Impact`). Made me guess wrong twice when filing PF-IMP-749. Either harmonize, or add a help-output note pointing to the divergence.
- feedback_db.py log-change has no update/delete subcommand. A typo in the IMP id field has no recourse other than logging a corrected duplicate, which leaves trend-analysis noise in the database. (See change #1013 vs #1014 from this session.)
- Validate-StateTracking.ps1's Get-MarkdownLinks helper didn't strip CommonMark `<URL>` angle-bracket syntax, causing false positives on links with spaces. Fixed in this session.

### Specific suggestions
- Rename either New-ProcessImprovement.ps1's `-Priority` to `-Impact`, or Update-ProcessImprovement.ps1's `-Impact` to `-Priority` (the field stores impact in the table). Pick one term.
- Add a `feedback_db.py update-change --id <#> --field <name> --value <new>` (or `delete-change --id <#>`) subcommand for typo correction.
- Consider adding a brief "Variant: User-directed in-session deviation" note to PF-TSK-009 Step 6 — when the user explicitly says "I want approach X but with deviation Y", capture how the framework handles that (it worked fine, but undocumented).
