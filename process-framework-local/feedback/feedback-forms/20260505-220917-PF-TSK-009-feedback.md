## Improvement Suggestions

### What worked well

- **Risk-class-driven Step 10** (low/medium/high) eliminated unnecessary per-change checkpoints for the two low-risk IMPs and the additive medium-risk one. Saved 4-6 round-trips this session.
- **Step 3 conciseness rule** + **Step 13 skip option** scaled the ceremony to the work. Three IMPs in a session would be impossible if each required full evaluation tables and full Step 13 diff restatements.
- **Step 2 problem verification** caught nothing this session (all 3 IMPs were valid) but the discipline of reading the actual artifact (appdev-20260505 temp state, PF-TSK-026 feedback form, PF-TSK-026 task definition) before evaluating prevented "trust the IMP description blindly" failure modes.
- **`New-Template.ps1` for the BlueprintSync template creation** auto-incremented PF-TEM (75→76), auto-added a PF-documentation-map.md entry, and gave PF-TEM-075 a frontmatter — all in one call. Saved ~3 manual edits.

### What could be improved

- **`New-Template.ps1` auto-placement of the doc-map entry** put the new "Temp Blueprint Sync State" entry at line 249, separated from the related `Temp* State` entries that cluster at lines 243-245. Title format also defaulted to "Temp" instead of the established "Temporary" prefix used by sibling entries. Both were trivial to fix manually but suggest the script's auto-placement could be smarter when adjacent entries follow a clear naming family.
- **Step 6 default framing for destructive recommendations** — my initial IMP-737 proposal phrased the no-references branch as "flag at Step 9 checkpoint as candidate for REMOVE" rather than "recommend REMOVE candidate; human decides per-case at Step 9." User had to refine. Both phrasings route through Step 9, but the human-facing tone differed in implied authority. PF-TSK-087's Framework Curator role explicitly addresses this for sync work, but the lesson is generic: when proposing edits to processes that affect destructive operations, default to recommend-not-decide framing.

### Specific suggestions

- (Doc-fixable, low priority) `New-Template.ps1` doc-map insertion could group new entries with existing entries that share a naming prefix (e.g., place "Template: Temporary X State" entries adjacent to other "Template: Temporary *" entries). Currently uses "append under section" semantics. Not blocking — manual reposition is 30 seconds.
- (Generic — not file-specific) When a Step 6 proposal touches destructive or asymmetric operations (delete, remove, classify-as-stale), the agent should explicitly route through "AI recommends, human decides per-case" framing in the proposal text rather than inferring it from per-task role descriptions. This is judgment, not codification — flagging as observation, not as IMP.
